TECHNICAL----------------------------------------------------------------------

Name: Ascension of the Rituals
Card Type: Rule
Rules Text: ① Ritual Monsters are put in the Extra Deck, not the Main Deck. ② Cards that Summon Ritual Monsters from the hand or Deck Summon them from the Extra Deck instead. Summoning conditions that require Ritual Monsters to be Summoned from the hand or Deck specify the Extra Deck instead. ③ Cards that add Ritual Monsters from the Deck to the hand, or move excavated Ritual Monsters, move Ritual Spells instead, based on how they're specified. (Excavated Ritual Spells that are moved to the field this way are Set.) ● By name or archetype: a Ritual Spell that Ritual Summons Ritual Monsters of that name or archetype, is of that archetype, or shares an archetype with that named Ritual Monster. ● Other (Attribute, Monster Type, etc.): a Ritual Spell that Ritual Summons either any Ritual Monster or only Ritual Monsters of that quality(s), or that shares an archetype with the activated card. ● Doesn't specify: any Ritual Spell. ④ Cards that send Ritual Monsters from the Deck to the GY or banishment send them from the Extra Deck instead. ⑤ Cards that reveal Ritual Monsters from the hand return them from the GY to the Extra Deck instead. Cards that reveal them from the Deck reveal them from the Extra Deck instead. ⑥ Cards that move Ritual Monsters from the hand to the GY or banishment (except by Tributing them) banish them from the GY instead. ⑦ Cards that Tribute Ritual Monsters from the hand Tribute them from the field instead. ⑧ Cards that equip Ritual Monsters from the hand or Deck to monsters on the field equip them from the GY or Extra Deck respectively instead. ⑨ Cards that move Ritual Monsters to the Deck, or to the hand (except from the Deck), move them to the Extra Deck instead. ⑩ Ritual Monsters that apply continous effects to themselves while in the hand or Deck apply those effects while in the Extra Deck instead. ⑪ Ritual Monsters, Ritual Spells, and cards that mention them or Ritual Summoning but not other Extra Deck monster card types or Summoning mechanics, that prevent a player(s) from Special Summoning monsters from the Extra Deck prevent non-Ritual Monsters from being Special Summoned from the Extra Deck instead, or if they apply a continuous effect to all of a player's monsters Special Summoned from the Extra Deck, they apply that effect to non-Ritual Monsters Special Summoned from the Extra Deck instead.

			- Exa, Enforcer of the Nekroz (③ SEARCH); Great Sorcerer of the Nekroz (③ SEARCH); and Shurit, Strategist of the Nekroz (③ SEARCH): Each adds 1 "Nekroz" Ritual Monster of the same type (Dragon, Spellcaster, and Warrior respectively) from the Deck to the hand. They can search "Nekroz" Ritual Spells or Ritual Spells that Ritual Summons "Nekroz" Ritual Monsters of only that type.
			- Nekroz of Sophia (② SUMMON ⑥ DISCRD ⑪ NOEDSS): It discards itself and 1 "Nekroz" Spell to prevent your opponent from Special Summoning monsters from the Extra Deck during this phase. This becomes discarding 1 "Nekroz" Spell and banishing itself from the GY to prevent your opponent from Special Summoning non-Ritual Monsters from the Extra Deck during this phase.
			- Nekroz of Valkyrus (⑥ DISCRD): It discards itself and banishes 1 "Nekroz" card from your GY to negate an attack and end the Battle Phase. This becomes banishing itself and 1 other "Nekroz" card from the GY.
		- Nouvelles:
			- Angry Burger (② SUMMON ⑤ REVEAL ⑨ RETRIV): It reveals itself from the hand to add 1 "Nouvelles" monster from your Deck to your hand, then shuffles itself into the Deck. Shuffling itself into the Deck would become returning itself to the Extra Deck, but that is redundant because revealing itself in the hand for the cost becomes returning itself from the GY to the Extra Deck, so ignore the self-shuffle part of the effect.
